# StudyBoost — Product Roadmap

The question bank is done: **14 subjects, 360+ authentic AP-style MCQs, every one
with an explanation, plus SM-2 review, mastery analytics, and per-user progress.**
That is already enough for a real student to use for weeks.

What decides the project's value now is **not** AP Biology Stage D — it is whether
a student who just answered their 20th question comes back the next day. For
college applications, the metric that matters most is **how many people use it
(reach / social impact)**, and reach depends on retention. So the roadmap is
ordered by retention ROI, not by feature completeness.

## P0 — Retention (must do first)

1. **Completion Screen** — results + next-step handoff after a practice set
2. **Review CTA** — primary button routing to the Mistake Log right after grading
3. **Streak Counter** — consecutive study days

These three close the core loop and are expected to move retention the most.

## P1 — Motivation (should do)

1. **Topic Mastery Visualization** — radar chart + progress bars
2. **Daily Progress Summary** — e.g.
   ```
   Today
   ✓ 18 questions completed
   ✓ 4 review cards finished
   ✓ +120 XP
   ```
3. **XP / Level system**

High-school students respond strongly to visible progress and XP.

## P2 — Acquisition (later)

1. **Landing page** with a clear 5-second value proposition
2. **First-time onboarding** flow
3. **Shareable progress cards** (achievement badges)

These improve conversion but are less important than closing the loop first.

## P3 — Social (future)

1. Leaderboards
2. Study groups
3. Class codes

## Governing rule

> **No new AP subjects until P0 is complete.**
> **No major question-bank expansion until the retention loop is validated.**

Deferred question-bank polish (e.g. AP World distractor strengthening) lives in
[BACKLOG.md](BACKLOG.md) and stays deferred under this rule.
